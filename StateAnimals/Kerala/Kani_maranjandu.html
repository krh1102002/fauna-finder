Kani maranjandu

Kingdom Animalia
Phylum Arthropoda
Sub-Phylum Crustacea
Class Malacostraca
Order Decapoda
InfraOrder Brachyura
Family Gecarcinucidae
Genus Kani
Species K. maranjandu

Kani maranjandu is a species of tree crab first identified in 2017. K. maranjandu has, to date, only been observed in the forests of the Western Ghats in Kerala, India. As of 2021, it is the only species in the genus Kani.

Kani maranjandu is distinguished from other crabs of the family Gecarcinidae by its distinctive carapace and the structure of the male abdomen, as well as its very long walking legs. The species is entirely arboreal, relying on water held in small hollows of trees for survival.

Kani maranjandu's type locality is the Thiruvananthapuram district of Kerala in the Western Ghats. It is endemic to this area.
